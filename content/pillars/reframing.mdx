# Pillar 2 — Reframing

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — Resistant  
**Function:** Name the emotion underneath the words. Redirect the meaning without dismissing it.

Most agents respond to resistance with information — more explanation, more features, more math. That is the wrong tool. Resistance is almost never intellectual. It is emotional. Fear of change. Fear of losing something. Fear of being wrong.

**You cannot explain someone out of a fear.**

A reframe works by doing the opposite: it names the fear, then redirects the meaning — without dismissing it, without arguing with it, and without logic.

---

## The Structure

> *"I hear you — [name what they're actually feeling]. [Redirect the meaning toward what this actually means for them]."*

The words change depending on the client. The structure does not. Validate the feeling. Redirect the meaning. Never dismiss, never argue, never logic first.

---

## Core Examples

**When the client says: "I don't want to change."**

❌ Wrong: Explaining why the new plan is better  
✅ Right: *"I hear you — you've had a plan that's worked and you don't want to risk losing what you have. What I'm showing you doesn't take that away. It adds to it."*

---

**When the client says: "I'm happy with what I have."**

❌ Wrong: *"But your plan is costing you money."*  
✅ Right: *"I hear you — and when something's worked for you, the last thing you want to do is mess with it. I'm not asking you to change anything. I'm asking you to look at the numbers. Give me 60 seconds and I'll show you exactly what's sitting unclaimed in your area."*

---

**When the client says: "I need to think about it."**

❌ Wrong: Accepting it and offering to call back  
✅ Right: *"I completely understand — and I want to make sure you have everything you need to think clearly. What's the specific part you're still working through? Because if it's about [doctor / cost / coverage], I can resolve that right now while we're on the phone."*

---

**When the client says: "I'll stick with what I got."**

❌ Wrong: Presenting more benefits  
✅ Right: *"I respect that. Here's the one thing I want to make sure you know before you do: you told me [thing they mentioned — dental, groceries, the premium]. Staying where you are means leaving $[annual amount] on the table every year. Not gaining something new — giving away something that's already funded for you. That's worth knowing. Can I show you the side-by-side?"*

---

## The Test Objection Technique

Before accepting an objection as real, test it:

> *"Absolutely — what questions would you be asking [whoever they want to consult] so I can make sure I'm better prepared for when we speak again?"*

The client will either name a question — which you answer right there — or they can't, and the deflection dissolves.

---

## What Reframing Is Not

Reframing is not:
- Dismissing what the client said ("I understand but...")
- Arguing with the client's belief
- Agreeing with the objection and then presenting more information anyway
- Repeating yourself louder or more slowly

Reframing is agreement + redirection. You agree with the *feeling*. You redirect the *meaning*.
