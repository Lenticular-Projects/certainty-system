# Pattern 7 — The Third Party Blind Spot

**Root Cause:** Wrong Response to Signal  
**Pillar Fix:** Persuasion (Pillar 1)  
**Signal when it happens:** 🟢 GREEN with gatekeeper / 🔴 RED

---

## What This Is

A family member, spouse, or caregiver is present on the call or in the background. The agent keeps selling to the primary caller. The real decision maker is never addressed. The decision gets made after the agent hangs up — and it goes the wrong way.

The sale wasn't lost because the math was wrong. It was lost because the conversation that decided it happened without the agent in the room.

---

## How to Recognize It Early

- *"I need to talk to my daughter first."*
- *"My husband handles all of this."*
- *"Let me ask my son — he set up my last plan."*
- You can hear another voice in the background giving instructions or commentary
- The client keeps pausing to relay what you're saying to someone else
- The client says yes — but hedges: *"I think that sounds good... let me just check with..."*

**Identify this in the first two minutes.** The longer you go without addressing it, the harder it becomes to get the third party on the call.

---

## The Fix — Get Them On the Call

The goal: before you hang up, the third party has heard the case directly from you.

> *"It sounds like [family member] is important to this decision — do you think they could jump on real quick? I'd love to walk both of you through this at the same time so no one has to play telephone afterward."*

If the third party is already in the room or on the line, address them directly:

> *"[Name], I want to make sure you have the full picture too — because this affects your [mom/dad/spouse] every month. Can I walk you through what I'm seeing real quick?"*

---

## If You Can't Get Them On

Arm the primary caller with the specific language they need to explain the decision:

> *"When [daughter/son] asks about this — and they will — here's exactly what you can tell them: 'A licensed agent ran the side-by-side comparison with me. My doctor is confirmed in-network. My medications are covered. And I'm getting [benefit] I wasn't getting before. They can call the agent directly if they want to go through it.'"*

This is also part of the Close Confirmation Protocol — arming every enrolled client with a script for the conversations that happen after you hang up.

---

## When the Objection Is *About* a Third Party

If the client says *"I want to talk to my daughter first"* — test it before accepting it:

> *"Absolutely — what questions would you be asking her so I can make sure I'm better prepared for when we speak again?"*

The client will either name a real question — which you answer right there — or they can't, and the deflection dissolves. If the daughter is genuinely involved, get her on the call now.
