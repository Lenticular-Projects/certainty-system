# How Your Calls Are Graded

Every call reviewed under The Certainty System receives a structured analysis using the **Certainty Score** — a 100-point framework built to tell you exactly what happened on a call, why it went the way it did, and what to do differently next time.

This is not a compliance audit. It is a sales coaching tool. Every point is backed by a specific timestamp and a one-sentence justification. You will never receive a score without knowing exactly why.

> **Scores are earned, not deducted.** The review starts at zero and adds points for what was present. A genuine attempt with imperfect execution earns partial credit. No attempt earns zero.

---

## Step 1 — Call Classification

Before scoring begins, every call is placed into one of four categories. The category determines how the call is reviewed and whether a score is applied.

**ENROLLED**
You closed. The audit focuses on execution quality — not just whether the client enrolled, but whether they hung up secure and certain. Did they know what plan they enrolled in? Could they explain why? Did you execute the Close Confirmation? A submitted enrollment without certainty is Pattern 9 (The Hollow Yes) in progress.

**MISSED OPPORTUNITY**
The plan was viable, the math made sense, no hard barriers existed — but the sale didn't close. This is the primary coaching category. The review names the exact failure pattern(s) and the root cause. This is where the most coaching value lives, because every Missed Opportunity was a call that could have gone differently.

**UNCLOSABLE**
A hard, logical barrier prevented the sale: a critical doctor out of network, a vital medication not covered, a Medicaid or SNP conflict that made the plan genuinely wrong for this client. Unclosable calls are not scored — there is no coaching value in scoring an outcome the agent could not have changed. The primary metric for Unclosable calls is **speed of diagnosis**: how quickly did the agent identify the barrier and handle the call professionally?

**CORRECT NO-SALE**
The agent correctly identified that the client was already on the optimal plan and recommended they stay. This is a skill, not a default — it requires the same plan knowledge and analytical ability as any other call type. Correct No-Sale calls are scored across 5 categories (Lead, Signal Reading, Math Breakdown, Objection Handling, Compliance & Professionalism). The raw score out of 90 is rescaled to 100 for display. Call Outcome Quality is not scored — the outcome is correct by definition.

---

## Step 2 — The Certainty Score

Enrolled and Missed Opportunity calls receive a **Certainty Score out of 100**, presented in the report immediately after the call title and before the narrative summary.

The score breaks down across six categories:

| Category | Max Points |
|---|---|
| Lead | 20 |
| Signal Reading | 20 |
| Math Breakdown | 20 |
| Objection Handling | 15 |
| Call Outcome Quality | 10 |
| Compliance & Professionalism | 15 |
| **TOTAL** | **100** |

---

## The Six Scoring Categories

---

### 1 — Lead (20 points)

*Did you lead the call, maintain that lead, and recover it when lost?*

This is the foundation. Every other category depends on it. An agent who loses the lead cannot execute the Math Breakdown, cannot handle objections, and cannot close.

**What earns points:**
- Held the authority and consultant frame throughout — stated and moved, never asked permission to advance
- Assumptive language used consistently — treated enrollment as the natural next step
- When the lead was lost, a deliberate recovery attempt was made before the call ended

**What costs points:**
- Permission-seeking language at any point: *"Is it okay if I..."*, *"I don't want to pressure you..."*
- Passive closes — asking the client if they want to enroll rather than moving toward it
- Lead lost and not recovered

**Score guide:** 18–20 = dominant frame throughout | 13–17 = held with minor slippage | 8–12 = lost lead at least once, partial recovery | 0–7 = lead lost early and not recovered

→ *See: [Pillar 1 — Persuasion](/pillars/persuasion), [Pattern 1 — Client Gold Ignored](/patterns/client-gold-ignored), [Pattern 4 — Permission-Seeking Language](/patterns/permission-seeking-language)*

---

### 2 — Signal Reading (20 points)

*Did you correctly identify what state the client was in at each key moment — and respond with the right tool?*

At every moment, the client is GREEN (open), RED (resistant), or YELLOW (drifting). Signal reading means deploying the correct pillar for the correct signal. Misreading the signal is Root Cause 2.

**What earns points:**
- Correct signal identified at each key moment in the call
- Right tool deployed — empathy first on RED, statement not question on YELLOW, value presentation on GREEN
- Client Gold recognized — emotionally significant things the client said were heard and deployed later

**What costs points:**
- Logic response to an emotional objection (RED moment treated as GREEN)
- Attempting to refocus a resistant client (YELLOW tool applied to RED signal)
- Missing Client Gold entirely — the client said something important and the agent moved past it

**Score guide:** 18–20 = every key moment correctly read | 13–17 = mostly correct, one misread | 8–12 = multiple misreads | 0–7 = signal reading absent

→ *See: [The Three Signals](/signals), [Pillar 2 — Reframing](/pillars/reframing), [Pillar 4 — Refocusing](/pillars/refocusing)*

---

### 3 — Math Breakdown (20 points)

*Was the Math Breakdown executed — and did it land?*

The Math Breakdown is three steps: State the comparison (specific numbers, not ranges), annualize it (say the annual figure out loud), humanize the cost of inaction (connect it to something specific this client said). Step 3 is the close. If it's missing, the breakdown didn't finish.

**What earns points:**
- Step 1 executed: specific monthly comparison stated
- Step 2 executed: annual figure stated explicitly — agent did not expect client to do the math
- Step 3 executed: annual number connected to something real this specific client said
- All three steps landed in sequence

**What costs points:**
- Benefit mentioned but not compared (no Step 1)
- Monthly number stated but not annualized (no Step 2)
- Annual number stated but not humanized — no connection to the client's specific life (no Step 3)
- Generic humanization — not connected to anything this particular client said

**Score guide:** 18–20 = all three steps executed cleanly | 13–17 = two steps complete, Step 3 incomplete | 8–12 = Step 1 only or Step 1+2 only | 0–7 = benefit mentioned but no structured breakdown

→ *See: [The Math Breakdown](/math-breakdown), [Pillar 3 — The Shift](/pillars/the-shift), [Pattern 2 — Incomplete Math Breakdown](/patterns/incomplete-math-breakdown)*

---

### 4 — Objection Handling (15 points)

*Was every objection handled with the correct tool for the signal being sent?*

The framework evaluates whether the agent deployed the right pillar at the right moment. A legitimate attempt requires both: acknowledgment of the emotion or concern (something specific — not just "I understand") AND a redirect toward value (a reframe, the Math Breakdown, or a forward-moving statement). Simply re-presenting the benefit after an objection is not a legitimate attempt.

**What earns points:**
- Correct tool deployed for the signal — Reframing on RED, Refocusing on YELLOW
- Stalls not validated — reframe attempted before accepting no
- Assertive but compliant — firm direction without pressure or aggression

**What costs points:**
- Logic response to an emotional objection
- Stall validated without reframe (*"Okay, call me back"*)
- Objection ignored entirely
- Aggressive or pressure-based language

**Score guide:** 13–15 = every objection handled with correct tool | 9–12 = most correct, one missed | 5–8 = multiple wrong tools | 0–4 = objections consistently ignored or validated

→ *See: [The Objection Handbook](/objections), [Pillar 2 — Reframing](/pillars/reframing), [Pattern 3 — Logic Responses](/patterns/logic-responses)*

---

### 5 — Call Outcome Quality (10 points)

*What happened at the moment of truth — and did the agent protect what they built?*

This category is evaluated differently based on call classification.

**For ENROLLED calls:**
- Close Confirmation attempted after enrollment (5 points) — Protocol delivered before hanging up. Client re-anchored to the specific reason they said yes, in their own words where possible.
- No unnecessary statements creating retention or compliance risk (5 points) — Nothing said that a manager would flag.

**For MISSED OPPORTUNITY calls:**
- Math Breakdown attempted at some level (5 points) — At least Step 1 executed.
- Recovery attempted at the losing moment (5 points) — After the call turned RED or the client moved toward exit, did the agent make a deliberate move to bring it back?

→ *See: [The Close Confirmation Protocol](/close-confirmation), [Pattern 9 — The Hollow Yes](/patterns/hollow-yes)*

---

### 6 — Compliance & Professionalism (15 points)

*Was the call run cleanly from a regulatory and professional standpoint?*

**What earns points:**
- TPMO disclaimer delivered correctly and completely (4 points)
- SOA confirmation obtained before plan discussion (4 points)
- Enrollment verification questions handled accurately (4 points)
- No statements that create regulatory or brand risk (3 points)

**What costs points:**
- Missing or incomplete compliance language
- Compliance language delivered in a way that killed sales momentum — read robotically at the wrong moment
- Statements on a recorded line that a compliance officer would flag
- Unnecessary statements that created risk or confusion

## What the Report Looks Like

Every reviewed call produces a written report with:

- **H1 Title:** Agent Name vs. Client Name
- **Certainty Score** displayed prominently before the narrative
- **Executive Summary:** 2–3 paragraph forensic narrative — call category, score, primary win or deal-breaker, root cause of any failure
- **Call Snapshot:** Clean table with agent, client, classification, call type, duration, enrollment result, plans, score
- **Score Breakdown:** Each of the 6 categories with timestamp-backed justification and one "to improve" action per category
- **What the Agent Did Right:** Specific, tactical wins with timestamps (2–5 items)
- **Failure Patterns Identified:** Each pattern named, timestamped, what it cost, exact words that would have prevented it
- **Math Breakdown Analysis:** Financial comparison table — either what was executed, or what it should have looked like
- **Call Type Diagnosis:** Which call type this was, whether it was correctly identified, what the correct opening move was
- **Objection Log:** Every objection, what the client said, what the agent did, correct or incorrect, what a legitimate attempt looks like
- **Close Confirmation Audit:** Was it executed, partially executed, or absent? Absent = Hollow Yes risk flagged
- **Full Timestamped Transcript**

---

## Difficulty Context

Call type difficulty is noted in every report but does not adjust the score mathematically. A Laydown Lead that scores 85 will be contextualized differently than a Scared Switcher that scores 85. The score reflects execution. The context reflects circumstances.

Every pattern identified in the report is solvable. That is not optimism — it is the design of the system.
