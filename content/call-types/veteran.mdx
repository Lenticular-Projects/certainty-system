# Type 8 — The Veteran

**Signal:** 🔴 RED (fear of displacement)  
**Primary Pillar:** The Shift  
**Biggest Mistake:** PCP selection before neutralizing VA fear

---

## Who This Is

They use VA benefits and see Medicare Advantage as a threat to what they've earned. They believe enrolling in a Medicare plan means replacing their VA care, losing their VA benefits, or making a choice that dishonors their service. None of that is true — but they don't know it yet.

If the agent jumps straight to plan mechanics or PCP selection before addressing this fear, the call ends. The moment a veteran hears "pick a primary care doctor," they hear "replace your VA doctor."

**Neutralize the displacement fear first. Everything else comes after.**

---

## How to Identify in the First 2 Minutes

- *"I get my care through the VA — I don't need this."*
- *"I'm a veteran. VA covers everything."*
- *"I already have VA benefits. Why would I need Medicare?"*
- Client hesitates or shuts down the moment PCP selection comes up.
- *"I'm not picking a different doctor. My VA doctor is my doctor."*

---

## Primary Move

Neutralize the displacement fear before presenting anything. VA and Medicare Advantage are additive, not competing. Establish this as the first thing they hear.

> *"Your VA covers everything at the VA. This covers everything outside the VA. They work together — this is additive, not a replacement. You're not choosing one over the other."*

Do not move to PCP selection, plan details, or math until this is understood and accepted.

---

## Common Objections on This Call

### "I get my care at the VA. I don't need Medicare."

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — displacement fear

**What's underneath:** They believe enrolling replaces or threatens VA coverage. This is the wall. Everything else waits.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- Jumping to PCP selection — the moment a veteran hears "pick a primary care doctor," they hear "replace your VA doctor"
- Explaining plan mechanics before addressing the fear — they stop listening the moment the fear isn't acknowledged

**✅ Option A:**
> *"Your VA care stays exactly as it is — nothing about this changes that. What Medicare Advantage covers is everything outside the VA: urgent care when you're traveling, specialists in the community, dental, OTC benefits, and emergency situations where getting to the VA isn't realistic. Think of it as a second layer that works alongside your VA coverage — not instead of it."*

**✅ Option B — connecting to real cost:**
> *"Here's the gap I want to show you: your VA covers you at VA facilities. Outside of that, you're exposed. An ER visit, a specialist your VA can't get you into fast enough, a procedure that requires a community provider — those costs land on you right now. This plan covers that gap at $0 premium. Your VA doesn't change. You just stop being exposed."*

---

### "I'm not picking a primary care doctor. My VA doctor is mine."

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — PCP resistance

**What's underneath:** The PCP requirement feels like being forced to abandon their VA provider. It is an administrative requirement, not a clinical replacement.

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I hear you — and your VA doctor stays your VA doctor. The PCP field in the enrollment is an administrative requirement for the system. It doesn't mean you're changing who you see. Your VA care is completely separate from this plan. Think of the PCP as a placeholder for the plan's records — it doesn't replace Dr. [VA doctor]. Would it help to pick someone close to you as a backup for non-VA situations?"*

---

### "I had a bad experience with Medicare before."

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — trust collapse from prior experience

**What's underneath:** A previous plan caused problems — billing issues, coverage gaps, VA disruption. They've grouped all Medicare Advantage into the same category.

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I hear you — and if something went wrong before, that's on whoever handled your enrollment. Can I ask you something? Did they check whether your VA care would be affected by the enrollment, or just whether the doctor was listed? Most agents skip that check. What I'm going to do right now is go through your VA coverage with you line by line to show you exactly what this plan touches — and what it doesn't."*

Then verify live. Show them that VA care is untouched. Let the system be the authority.

---

## From the Calls

An agent jumped straight to PCP selection on a VA client before addressing the displacement fear. The client shut down completely. The enrollment was over. Another agent used the additive framing — *"Your VA covers the VA. This covers everything else. They stack."* — and the veteran said "Why has no one ever explained it that way?" He enrolled that day.

**The moment you mention PCP before neutralizing the VA fear, the call is over.**
