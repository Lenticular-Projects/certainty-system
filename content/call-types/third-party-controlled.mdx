# Type 4 — The Third Party Controlled Call

**Signal:** 🟢 GREEN + gatekeeper  
**Primary Pillar:** Persuasion  
**Biggest Mistake:** Selling to the wrong person

---

## Who This Is

The person on the phone is not the one making the decision. A spouse, adult child, sibling, or caregiver holds the real authority. The client will say yes — and then check with that person after the agent hangs up. That conversation, without the agent in it, is where the sale dies.

The goal: before hanging up, the third party has heard the case directly from the agent. Either get them on the call now, or arm the primary caller with the exact language they need for that conversation.

---

## How to Identify in the First 2 Minutes

- *"I need to talk to my daughter first."*
- *"My son handles all my financial stuff."*
- *"I want to check with my husband."*
- *"Let me have my son call you back."*
- *"I don't make any decisions without my sister."*
- *"My caregiver helps me with this kind of thing."*

---

## Primary Move

Identify the real decision maker early. Get them on the call — or sell to both simultaneously.

> *"I completely understand. Is [daughter/son/spouse] available right now? It would take less than 10 minutes and it's easier than playing phone tag — that way they can hear everything directly and ask me any questions."*

If they cannot get on the call now, arm the primary caller:

> *"When you talk to [them], here's exactly what to say: 'A licensed agent ran the side-by-side comparison with me. My doctor is confirmed in-network. My medications are covered. The benefit comes to $[amount] a year. They can call the agent directly if they want to go through it.'"*

---

## Common Objections on This Call

### "I need to talk to my daughter / son first."

**Signal:** 🟢 GREEN with gatekeeper

**What's underneath:** They are interested. They just have deferred the final say to someone else. This is often protective instinct — they do not want to make a mistake that the family member will question.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- *"Of course — have them give me a call."* — the agent is now out of the conversation
- Scheduling a callback without arming the caller — they will have a conversation you are not in
- Agreeing to send information "to look over" — the sale dies in the mailbox

**✅ Option A — get the third party on now:**
> *"I completely understand — and I actually think that's the right call. Is [name] available right now? It would take less than 10 minutes and it's much easier than playing phone tag. That way they can hear everything directly from me and ask any questions they have."*

**✅ Option B — arm the caller if the third party isn't available:**
> *"Absolutely. When you talk to [name], here's what I want you to be able to tell them: 'I verified my doctor is in-network. My medications are covered. The plan pays $[amount] a year more than what I have now.' Write that down — those are the three things they'll want to know. And here's my direct number if they want to call me."*

---

### Third party joins and is skeptical.

**Signal:** 🔴 RED from the third party

**What's underneath:** They're protecting their family member. They didn't hear the full call. They're starting from zero — and they may have heard previous scam calls.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- Being defensive or dismissive of their skepticism
- Trying to rush through the case because the call is already long
- Addressing the primary caller while the third party is on — talk to the room

**✅ Correct Response:**
Greet the third party directly. Start fresh with them:
> *"[Name], I'm glad you could join — your [mother/father] made a great call to include you. Let me take 90 seconds and run through exactly what we found, because I'd rather you hear it directly than second-hand. [Primary caller's] doctor [name] is confirmed in-network. Their medications are covered. The plan we're looking at pays $[amount] more per year than what they currently have. Does that math make sense to you, or do you want me to walk through any part of it?"*

Turn the skeptic into an ally by treating them as a participant, not an obstacle.

---

### "My spouse handles the finances. They're not home right now."

**Signal:** 🟢 GREEN with timing constraint

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"Got it — when is [spouse] usually home? I'd love to get them on a quick call together so I can walk you both through it at the same time. It takes less than 10 minutes and I can answer their questions directly. What time works?"*

If scheduling: lock a specific time. Do not say "have them call me." You set the appointment.

---

## From the Calls

A client who was interested, said yes, then mentioned she'd need to "run it by her son." The agent said "no problem, have him call." The son never called — and the client disenrolled two weeks later because the son had concerns nobody had addressed. The enrollment was real. The close confirmation was not complete.

**Getting the third party on the call is not optional. It is the difference between an enrollment and a disenrollment.**
