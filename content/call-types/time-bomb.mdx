# Type 6 — The Time Bomb

**Signal:** 🟢 GREEN + countdown  
**Primary Pillar:** Refocusing  
**Biggest Mistake:** Spending time on rapport when the clock is running

---

## Who This Is

They're open and motivated — but they have a hard constraint. A deadline, an upcoming medical procedure, a caregiver arriving, a ride coming in 10 minutes. The urgency is real and it can work for you or against you. The mistake is not recognizing it and treating this call like a standard enrollment.

---

## How to Identify in the First 2 Minutes

- *"I only have a few minutes — my ride is coming."*
- *"I have a doctor's appointment this afternoon."*
- *"I don't have a lot of time today."*
- *"I have a procedure scheduled next month."*
- *"My enrollment deadline is coming up."*
- *"I'm not feeling well — can we make this quick?"*

---

## Primary Move

Identify the constraint immediately. Acknowledge it. Then use it as the reason to accelerate — not the reason to reschedule.

> *"I hear you — we don't have much time. Here's what I want to do: I'll skip everything that's not essential and take you straight to the number. Tell me the one benefit you care most about and I'll show you if it's available for your zip code. That takes 4 minutes. If it works, we can get you set up before your ride comes."*

---

## Common Objections on This Call

### "I only have a few minutes / my ride is coming."

**Signal:** 🟢 GREEN with time constraint

**What's underneath:** They called because they wanted to call. The time constraint is real — use it as urgency, not as a reason to end the call.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- *"No problem — should I call you back?"* — you lose the momentum that got them to call
- Spending any time on rapport or discovery that doesn't accelerate toward the close
- Ignoring the constraint and running a full-length call — they will have to hang up on you

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"Perfect — I'll make this fast. Tell me the one thing you most want out of a new plan and I'll go straight there. We can get this done before your ride comes."*

Then move directly to the most relevant benefit, run the minimum viable Math Breakdown (Steps 1 and 2 at minimum), and go for the close. Do not linger.

If they need to hang up before enrollment: *"I've got everything pulled up. When can you call back — do you have 5 minutes tomorrow morning? Because the work is already done."*

---

### "I'm not feeling well / I'm tired."

**Signal:** 🟡 YELLOW — energy constraint

**What's underneath:** They called in a window of motivation. That window is closing. This is not the moment for rapport.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- *"Oh no, I hope you feel better! Let's reschedule."* — losing the window
- A long call that exhausts them further

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I completely understand — and I don't want to make this harder on you. Here's where we are: I've already confirmed your doctor is covered and the benefit comes out to $[amount] a year. When you're feeling better, we can finish this in under 5 minutes because the work is already done. What time tomorrow would be best?"*

Do not restart from zero on the callback. Open with: *"I've got everything pulled up from when we spoke. Your doctor [name] is in-network and the plan pays $[amount]. Let's pick up where we left off."*

---

### "I have a procedure coming up — I don't want to change anything right now."

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — timing fear

**What's underneath:** They're worried switching will disrupt their upcoming medical care. This is legitimate — but often unfounded. The coverage date is what matters, not the call date.

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"That's exactly the right concern to have — and I want to check something with you. Have you verified your surgeon is covered under your current plan? Because that's the one thing that actually matters for your procedure."*

If you run the check and the surgeon is covered on the new plan:
> *"Your surgeon is in-network on both plans. What changes is that you're also picking up [benefit]. The procedure is covered. The worry is resolved. We can get this done in 5 minutes."*

---

## From the Calls

A client called with 10 minutes before her ride arrived. She was motivated. The agent spent the first 5 minutes on rapport and discovery. When the ride arrived, the client had to hang up. There was no follow-up. The call — and the enrollment — was lost to a time constraint the agent created by not accelerating.

**The Time Bomb calls you about a benefit. The clock is an ally if you use it. It becomes the enemy if you ignore it.**
