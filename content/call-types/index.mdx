# The 9 Call Types

> **In the first two minutes of every call, you are doing one thing: pattern recognition.** The type tells you the playbook. The playbook tells you what's coming before it arrives. You are not reacting to a new situation — you are deploying a known response to a pattern you have already seen.

Most calls are combinations of types. Identify the dominant one first and lead from there.

## The Two You Must Know Cold

**Types 1 and 2 are the most common calls you will receive.** Based on actual call data, the Scared Switcher (doesn't want to change) and the Money Caller (calling about the benefit) are the dominant types. Know these two without needing to look anything up.

---

## How the Call Types Relate to the System

The Four Pillars tell you *how* to respond. The 9 Call Types tell you *who* you're talking to — before the first objection has been raised.

The call types are the **proactive layer** of the system. The Nine Patterns are diagnostic — they explain what went wrong after a call is lost. The call types are preventive — they tell you what to do before anything goes wrong.

Together they form a complete loop: you enter the call with a playbook, and when something breaks down, you have a pattern name for what happened and a specific fix for it.
