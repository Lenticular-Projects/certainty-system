# Type 5 — The Detail Staller

**Signal:** 🟢/🔴 mix  
**Primary Pillar:** The Shift  
**Biggest Mistake:** Giving them something to research

---

## Who This Is

They want more information before deciding. They are not resistant — they are deferring. The words sound like curiosity but the behavior is avoidance. They will request plan documents, a website, a plan ID number, or a callback when they've "had time to look it over."

The problem: every one of those requests removes the agent from the conversation and puts the client alone with their doubts and whoever else they happen to talk to. **Giving a staller more information does not help them decide. It gives them more things to question.**

---

## How to Identify in the First 2 Minutes

- *"Can you send me something to look over?"*
- *"I want to think about it."*
- *"What's the plan ID so I can look it up?"*
- *"I'd like to read the materials before I do anything."*
- *"Let me do a little research and call you back."*
- *"I just want to make sure I'm making the right decision."*

---

## Primary Move

Make inaction expensive in specific dollars. Then close.

Do not provide the plan ID. Do not agree to mail documents. Do not schedule a callback without a specific number on the table that makes waiting cost something.

> *"I completely understand wanting to be sure — and I want to make sure you're making the right call too. Here's what I want you to know before we hang up: every month we wait on this, that's $[monthly benefit] that doesn't come back. $[annual] this year that you don't get. I'm not asking you to make a permanent decision — I'm asking you to not leave $[amount] on the table. Let's take 3 more minutes and make sure the numbers work. If they don't, I'll tell you."*

---

## Common Objections on This Call

### "I want to think about it."

**Signal:** 🟢/🔴 mix — classic stall

**What's underneath:** They don't have a specific objection. They have ambient discomfort with committing. This often means the agent has not yet made inaction expensive enough.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- *"Of course — take your time."* — you are agreeing with the stall
- *"I'll call you back next week."* — you lose control of timing and follow-through
- Asking what they need to think about without giving them a number first — opens an unlimited objection window

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I completely hear you — and the reason I want to keep going for 2 more minutes isn't to pressure you. It's because I want you to know what you're thinking about. Right now, you have $[amount] available that you're not getting. Every month you think about it, that's $[monthly]. That's real money. If the answer after 2 more minutes is still no, I respect that — but at least you'll know what the no costs you."*

---

### "Can you send me the plan documents to look over?"

**Signal:** 🟢/🔴 — exit request disguised as interest

**What's underneath:** They want to exit the call without saying no. Sending documents is a polite way to defer.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- *"Sure, what's your email?"* — you are handing them the exit
- *"I'll put something in the mail."* — the sale dies in their mailbox

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I can absolutely get you the plan documents — and I want to make sure they're meaningful when you read them. The one thing those documents can't tell you is the comparison to what you have now. That's what I'm here for. Give me 3 more minutes and I'll walk you through the side-by-side so when you read the documents, you know exactly what you're looking at."*

---

### "Give me the plan ID so I can look it up."

**Signal:** 🔴 RED — exit with a task

**What's underneath:** They want a callback mechanism that never happens. Most clients who request a plan ID to "look it up" do not look it up.

**❌ What NOT to say:**
- Just giving the plan ID and ending the call — you are done
- Giving the website and saying "everything is on there" — same result

**✅ Correct Response:**
> *"I'll get you everything you need before we hang up — and I want to make sure it's useful when you have it. The challenge with looking it up on your own is that the plan page doesn't show you the comparison to your current plan, and it doesn't run your specific doctors or medications. That's what I can show you right now. Give me 3 minutes on that first — then I'll give you everything."*

---

## From the Calls

Every agent who has agreed to "send something" and scheduled a callback has experienced the same thing: the client does not call back. The information arrives. It sits. The opportunity closes. The one thing that was working was the agent on the phone with the client making the numbers real. That ends the moment you agree to a callback without making inaction expensive first.
